A Passion is Born

My journey with photography began 14 years ago when I transferred schools and moved from my native St. Louis to Chicago in the middle of winter and the start of the Spring, 2007 Semester. While I pretty much loathed the city from early on, a couple life-changing things did happen to me: My roommate gave me his old camera and showed pictured from Yellowstone National Park-a place he worked the previous summer and I used to go to with my family as a kid but had long forgotten about.

To stay sane and get through the rest of the semester Shane and I would explore the city whenever we got a chance, photographing the people and whatever else caught our eye. As soon as the semester ended however, I packed up and moved to Yellowstone for the summer and my love for adventure and the American West was reborn! I was infatuated with the freedom of the mountain and would spend my free time hiking and exploring the park taking pictures of waterfalls, wolfs, sunsets, etc.

The summer eventually came and ended and by chance that winter was my parents 25th wedding anniversary, for which they took my sisters and I to Belize. In the brief two weeks that we were there however, my fascination for mountains and adventure was extended to the entire world and different cultures.

I returned to the States and worked another season in Yellowstone, this time as a raft guide, where I proceeded to befriend a group of Colombians as-I already spoke a little Spanish- that were there for the summer to work. That combined with inspiration to travel from my boss who was also a wildlife photographer and had been all around the world, and my head guide who was South African and whom had been also been to countless countries and I decided that winter to travel through Latin America without any plans of coming back.

Getting Lost in the World and Discovering Culture

I boarded a plan to San Jose, Costa Rica a couple months later and spent the next couple months traveling through Costa Rica, Panama and eventually even sailed to Colombia, a country I quickly fell in love with, and spent the next 4 months there exploring everything from the mountains to the sun soaked beaches of the Caribbean and in that time realized how much I loved not only getting lost in world and discovering something new every day, but also how much I had grown to love meeting new people and exploring diverse cultures and how photography was also a means to delve beyond simple talking briefly with someone, but was rather a way to connect with people and their customs and create real connections in a place where I would otherwise simply be looked at as a foreigner.




The Adventure Continues

When the Spring started to roll around again, I longed to be back in the mountains of Montana and with my friends rafting on the river and fishing or hiking on our time off. By the time the summer ended and the cold Autumn winds started to blow however I started to dream of where else in the world I could go explore with my camera and I took off yet again to Colombia with a friend and ended up staying another 4 months.
